The 42nd Midwinter 10 Mile Classic was pushed back to March because of forecasted tides, wind, and flooding risk, but Dirigo still opened the season with a deep result sheet in Cape Elizabeth.

Karley Piers won the women's race in 1:00:02, with Clíodhna O'Malley right behind in 1:00:16 to give Dirigo the top two women's places. Veronica Graziano won F35-39 in 1:01:57, Maria Remillard was third in F25-29 in 1:04:46, Michelle Lilienthal was second in F40-44 in 1:06:51, Maddie Sheehan ran 1:06:54, and Paula Rousseau was second in F60-64 in 1:24:29.

On the men's side, Jon McGonagle ran 1:01:34, Craig Robinson 1:01:49, Nedicus Swain 1:03:22, Peter Burke won M55-59 in 1:04:57, David Ouellette ran 1:05:56, Nevin Duffey 1:09:20, and Christian Johnson 1:17:08.

The women's depth stood out as much as the win: Dirigo put six women in the top 80 overall.
