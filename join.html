Dirigo membership is open to runners who want to be part of the team. Annual dues are $40.

Membership Fit

Who should consider Dirigo?

Membership is open to runners who want to be part of the team. Older versions of Dirigo materials referenced qualifying guidelines, but the current public-facing posture should be welcoming first, then clear about racing, team scoring, and volunteer expectations where they apply.

Membership Process

Make the next step obvious.

1

Reach out

Send the membership form so the club has your name, email, running background, and what you are looking for.

2

Get a follow-up

A club representative can confirm next steps, answer questions, and explain how dues and team communications work.

3

Pay dues and get set up

Annual dues are $40. After the club follows up, members can complete payment and get added to team communications.

Membership Details

The essentials, stated plainly.

Standards

Qualifying standards are no longer presented as a gate for joining. Racing expectations may still apply for team scoring or specific events.

Dues

Annual membership dues are $40.

Benefits

Membership connects runners to the Dirigo team, club communications, racing opportunities, and the broader Maine running community.

USATF

USATF membership may be needed for team scoring or championship events. The club can confirm what applies after a member joins.

Prospective Member FAQ

Questions people should not need to email about.

Do I need to meet a time standard?

No. Membership is open to runners who want to be part of the team. Specific races or team scoring opportunities may have their own requirements.

Can I come to a group run before joining?

Yes, when a run is listed as public. Check current details first, since some team activity may be informal, seasonal, or race-specific.

What does membership include?

Team membership, club communications, racing coordination, and connection to other Maine runners. Dues are $40 per year.
