History

Maine's racing team, built on the road.

Dirigo began in 2002 with a simple idea: bring Maine runners together to compete as a team in New England's deepest races.

Origin

It started on Flying Point Road.

The name came during a hard road workout in Freeport. Andy Spaulding, Todd Coffin, and Barry Logan were running intervals near Flying Point Road when Spaulding offered the word that would stick: Dirigo.

It fit. Dirigo is Maine's state motto, Latin for "I lead," and the new club's purpose was direct: give Maine a strong team that could line up in the USATF New England Grand Prix and see how Maine runners stacked up against the region.

Source: John Rolfe, "Maine's premier team? Look under 'Dirigo RC'," March 24, 2002.

Timeline

Milestones worth preserving.

Mar. 2002

The name takes shape

During a Freeport workout, the founders settled on Dirigo, Maine's motto and a fitting identity for a club built around representing the state in competition.

Mar. 2002

New Bedford debut

Dirigo made its New England Grand Prix debut at the New Bedford Half-Marathon, placing fifth among 20 teams with a top-five average under 1:11.

2002

Blue and black on the roads

The early uniforms were blue singlets and black shorts, with a conifer logo adapted from an old Steve Prefontaine jersey.
